Wait—that's the same number. But let me translate what this actually means for a company of this size:

| Metric | Your Presentation | Reality |
|--------|------------------|---------|
| Operating Cash Inflow | "127.99B cash generated" | Total cash collected from customers |
| Operating Cash Outflow | Ignored | **494.75B paid to suppliers/employees** |

**This is the real story:** The company has **80.99 billion yuan in current liabilities** that need to be refinanced or paid within the next 12 months. The 78.5% debt ratio isn't "industry standard" for a healthy company—it's a company **living on the edge of refinancing risk**. Every time credit markets tighten, every time a major customer delays payments, every time interest rates rise, this leverage becomes more dangerous.

**What does high leverage mean in practice?** It means when revenue inevitably slows—and it will with consumer electronics—it means reduced flexibility to weather downturns. It means management making short-term decisions to preserve cash rather than long-term investments. It means being forced to cut dividends or dilute shares when conditions turn.

The bull's Tesla comparison fails for another reason: **Tesla was growing into its debt with exponentially increasing margins. Crystal Opto is maintaining margins in a maturing market with intensifying competition.** That's not the same story.
